Celeste Community Difficulty Ratings

(CCDR)

Tired of map difficulty rankings that tell you nothing about whether you might actually enjoy a map?

Enter the CCDR

A CCDR rating is made up of 2 or 4 numbers from 1-5

These numbers correspond to Tech Complexity / Precision Required / Chocolatey-ness / Sight-unreadability

A CCDR badge can display either tech complexity & precision:

Or all 4 values:

For reference, the CCDR for all vanilla A-Sides would be 1/1/1/1, where something like [map] would be [complex rating]

So, what the hell are these numbers?

Tech Complexity

This is probably the most subjective category here. Basically, this number corresponds to the kinds of tech that need to be used. A 1 here would correspond to tech learned in vanilla A/B sides, a 2 would be vanilla C-sides/Farewell, et cetera. A 5 would mean a map makes heavy use of tech on the level of [insane tech]

Precision Required

This is basically a rating of how unforgiving the map is, where a 1 essentially corresponds to a vanilla a-side, and 5 means most things require a setup.

Chocolatey-ness

The name of this category comes from the standard phrasing of "vanilla" and "chocolate" to give a name to how many helper mods a map uses. 1 here would mean only vanilla mechanics are used, 5 would mean a significant number of mods are used. Something like Mindcrack would be a 4 or 5 on this scale.

Sight-unreadability

This was the hardest to name (dm me if you have a better name lmao), but basically this could be considered a rating of "puzzliness" -- 1 being "it's obvious what to do as soon as you see it", and 5 being "Getting through this is obscure af". A 5 here does NOT mean the actual execution is difficult, only that figuring out what to do is.

Alright, cool... now how do I use it?

For right now, the CCDR is really just this numbering scheme; Our fancy badges will be coming soon, and, in the future, I want it to be a true community rating system. It will support feedback and everything, but I'm making this as a proof-of-concept so I can see if people actually like the system
